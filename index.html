Smart Fish Route Predictor: A Multi-Module Machine Learning Framework

AI-Driven Fisheries Decision Support Using Oceanography, Local Catch Intelligence, Market Trends, and Ecological Screening

Explore the Research Meet the Team

Research Objective: Smart Fish Route Predictor is a multi-module machine learning framework designed to support fisheries decision-making in small-scale fishing communities. By integrating oceanographic hotspot prediction, spawning-likelihood screening, market trend forecasting, and hyperlocal catch-success prediction, the system helps fishers decide where to fish, when to land catches, and when to apply ecological caution.

Core Framework Modules

Four interconnected intelligence engines driving sustainable fishing decisions.

🌊

Oceanographic Hotspot Prediction

Identifies high-potential fishing zones across the Indian Ocean using XGBoost classifiers and environmental telemetry.

  • SST, SSS, Chlorophyll-a analysis
  • 0.5-degree spatial grid prediction
  • Supports "Where to fish" decisions
🐟

Proxy Spawning-Likelihood Screening

Flags ecologically sensitive areas for conservation-aware harvesting based on complex environmental proxy indicators.

  • Multi-variate ecological modeling
  • Spawning-favorable area screening
  • Adds ecological caution signals
📈

Market Trend Forecasting

Forecasts fish price trends at major markets to support landing-time optimization for better economic returns.

  • Peliyagoda Market data (2019-2025)
  • XGBoost & Ensemble Regressors
  • Optimizes landing time value
📍

Hyperlocal Catch-Success Prediction

Predicts local catch potential at the community level using CPUE data and localized weather patterns.

  • Focus: Point Pedro, Northern Sri Lanka
  • Wind, Pressure & Local CPUE basis
  • Highly localized trip guidance

Project Domain & Methodology

Bridging machine learning and sustainable fisheries management.

Literature Survey

Traditional fisheries forecasting has relied heavily on habitat suitability models, linear regression, and ARIMA-style approaches. However, modern fisheries demand more robust methods capable of modeling the complex, non-linear interactions among oceanographic shifts, fish movement patterns, and volatile market behaviors that classical methods often fail to capture.

Research Gap

Most existing studies focus on isolated tasks: either hotspot mapping, biological spawning analysis, market prediction, or local catch modeling. Very few initiatives have successfully integrated these multi-dimensional factors into a single, coordinated framework capable of providing holistic decision support for small-scale fisheries.

Research Problem

Small-scale fishers require practical, real-time decision support to navigate rapidly changing ocean conditions and volatile economic environments. Currently, available tools are fragmented, leaving fishers to rely on intuition rather than a combined stream of spatial, ecological, and economic intelligence.

Research Objectives

  • ✓ To identify high-potential fishing hotspots using oceanographic and catch data.
  • ✓ To forecast short-term fish market trends for landing-time optimization.
  • ✓ To screen potentially sensitive spawning-favorable areas using proxy ecological indicators.
  • ✓ To predict hyperlocal catch success using Point Pedro CPUE and local environmental conditions.
  • ✓ To integrate all modules into a unified fisheries decision-support framework.

Methodology

The system utilizes a structured data pipeline: data is harvested from diverse sources (satellite oceanography, market records, and manual fishing logs), followed by intensive preprocessing and cyclic feature engineering. XGBoost serves as our central machine learning backbone, tuned specifically for each module's unique data distribution. The final output is an integrated advisory workflow that translates model probabilities into actionable fishing routes.
